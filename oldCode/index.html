Sharing the beauty of kimono with the world

Kimono きもの

This kimono shop was opened with the desire to share one of the most beautiful forms of traditional clothing--the kimono--with the world.

Over twenty years' time acting as a homestay family for travelers from all over the world, I received numerous requests for kimono fittings, shared outings where kimono are traditionally worn, as well as several orders for kimono themselves. With that experience in mind, I began volunteering as a cultural envoy, and eventually decided to open a store of my own.

I invite you, travelers to Japan, to bring your memories of Japan home—to display authentic kimono in your home, to share with children, perhaps even wear to parties or as a costume in your home country.

Antique Kimono - Romina's Shopping Experience

The feeling of owning your own kimono for only a few thousand yen

Romina came to visit Japan recently, from Germany.
While browsing together at the fleemarket, she mentioned that she would have liked to buy a kimono, but none were available. I happen to collect certain antiques, so I took her to my warehouse to show her some of mine; when she saw it her eyes went wide with excitement. She bought five kimono before heading home to Germany.

I hear a lot of my homestay guests say things like, "I'd really like to try on a haori jacket," "I love kimono, but would really like to buy old kimono fabric to make western clothes. But, no matter where I go I have a hard time finding good, reasonablely priced, used kimono," and so on. They would urge me to open an online shop, or even start a mail order company. "In Germany, even old used kimono sell for tens of thousands of yen. If it were possible to get them for cheaper, I would be so happy." So, today we are working on starting a mail order company in Germany.


Actual kimono in person are often more vivid and reveal much more fineness in their pattern than is captured in photographs. Please stop by our shop to have a closer look; it would be our pleasure to share them with you.
